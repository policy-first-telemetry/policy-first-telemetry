The problem

Auditors want evidence. Employees want privacy. Existing solutions often force a bad trade‑off: heavyweight security suites on one side, invasive “employee monitoring” tools on the other.

Overkill platforms

Powerful, expensive, and complex — often too heavy for mid‑market teams and narrow audit needs.

Surveillance tools

Screenshots, keystrokes, and content capture create legal/HR risk and destroy trust.

✓

Defensible by default

Minimize first. Collect only what policy allows.

⚖

Privacy‑first posture

Designed to avoid “surveillance” categories and associated risk.

⏱

Audit‑ready outputs

Searchable events, exports, and access logs (planned).

What it is

A lightweight endpoint agent + admin policy controls + reporting UI that produces policy‑approved browser telemetry for audit/compliance workflows.

How it works

  1. Admin sets a policy (domain‑only vs full URL, exclusions, redaction rules, hours, retention).
  2. Agent collects only what policy allows (active tab only).
  3. Data is searchable and exportable for audits (with role‑based access; planned).

What it is not

This is not a productivity tracker and not an employee surveillance tool. The design intentionally avoids capturing content or sensitive signals.

No content capture

No screenshots, no text extraction, no page content.

No behavioral inference

No “productivity” scoring, no user behavior profiling.

No background activity

No background‑tab content capture. Active tab only.

No full history rebuild

Not designed to reconstruct full browsing history.

Policy‑first collection

“Policy‑first” means raw signals are immediately transformed into policy‑approved events: minimize first, redact, exclude — before anything is stored or uploaded.
